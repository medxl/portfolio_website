Eary life

Adam Smith's journey began in the lively streets of Lagos, Nigeria, where he was born on November 1, 1995. From a young age, Adam exhibited a natural curiosity and boundless energy that set him apart. Growing up in the vibrant cityscape of Lagos, he developed a deep appreciation for the diversity and dynamism that surrounded him. Even in his early years, Adam's fascination with the mysteries of the human body became evident. This early spark led him down the path to medical school, where he embraced the challenges of anatomy, physiology, and the intricacies of healthcare with enthusiasm. His peers quickly recognized not only his academic prowess but also his ability to inject humor into the often-stressful world of medical studies. In the hallowed halls of academia, Adam's laughter echoed during late-night study sessions, providing a refreshing break from the intensity of medical coursework. Despite the demanding nature of his studies, he navigated through textbooks and cadaver labs with a spirit that hinted at a broader spectrum of interests beyond medicine. However, the unexpected twist in Adam's narrative emerged when he discovered a new passion: coding. Somewhere between medical lectures and clinical rotations, the world of algorithms and programming languages beckoned. What started as a casual exploration soon transformed into a captivating hobby. Late nights once reserved for medical textbooks now became playgrounds for experimenting with lines of code. Amidst jokes from friends about being the "coding doc," Adam found a unique synergy between his medical studies and newfound interest in coding. The problem-solving skills honed in medical school seamlessly translated into the logical thinking required for programming. As he delved into the dual worlds of medicine and coding, Adam discovered the beauty of embracing diverse passions.
